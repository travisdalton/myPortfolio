About Me

I was born in Pawtucket, RI and grew up moving fairly frequently until landing in the Valley Forge/Wayne, Pennsylvania for the latter part of my youth and most of my young adult life. I later attended the University of Delaware. This was followed by an assortment of work experiences and eventually I wound up attending Suffolk University Law School in Boston.

In the years following law school, I worked an assortment of jobs before landing in my current role where I learned I had an interest in technology - specifically development and coding.

On a personal front, my wife Marissa and I married in September of 2019 and we are expecting our first child in April 2020. Fittingly it's on Masters Sunday - the greatest Sunday of the year.

I didn’t think the upcoming birth of my first child was going to be enough work, so I decided that it was the right time to learn how to code and enrolled in Bloc. I find coding and development to be fascinating and extremely rewarding. It is essentially solving one problem after the next. Law school helped me develop the ability to break big problems down into smaller problems. Once you’ve solved all of those smaller problems, the solution falls into place for the big problem. It’s like putting together a puzzle and I get a real charge out of it. I look forward to my next adventure in development.

Thanks for reading a bit about me. Please feel free to get in touch!
